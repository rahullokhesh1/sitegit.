Back Exercises

Deadlifts

Lift a loaded barbell or weights from the ground to a standing position, engaging the lower back, glutes, and hamstrings.

Bent-Over Rows

Bend at the hips while holding a barbell or dumbbells, then pull the weight toward your lower chest or abdomen to target the middle and upper back.

Lat Pulldowns

Use a cable machine to pull a bar down to your chest, effectively developing the lats (latissimus dorsi) in your upper back.

Pull-Ups

Hang from a bar and pull your body upward until your chin clears the bar, engaging the upper back and lats.

T-Bar Rows

Lift a loaded barbell using a T-Bar row machine or a landmine attachment, targeting the middle and upper back muscles.

Seated Cable Rows

Sit at a cable machine, use a V-bar attachment, and pull the handle towards your torso to isolate the middle back.

Face Pulls

Pull a rope attachment towards your face on a cable machine to work the rear deltoids and upper traps.

Chin-Ups

Similar to pull-ups but with palms facing you, targeting the upper back and lats.

Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows

Brace one hand and knee on a bench while rowing a dumbbell with the other hand, working each side of the back individually.

Hyperextensions or Back Extensions

Perform on a specialized bench to strengthen the lower back muscles and erector spinae.
