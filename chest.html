Chest Exercises

1. Bench Press:

Lie on a flat bench with a barbell or dumbbells.
Lower the weight to your chest and push it back up.
This exercise targets the chest, particularly the pectoralis major.



2. Dumbbell Flyes:

Lie on a bench with dumbbells in each hand.
Start with your arms extended above your chest.
Lower your arms out to the sides in a wide arc and then bring them back up.
This exercise targets the chest muscles.


3. Incline Bench Press:

Lie on an inclined bench (angled upwards) with a barbell or dumbbells.
Lower the weight to your upper chest and push it back up.
This exercise emphasizes the upper chest muscles.



4.Chest Dips:

Use parallel bars or dip station handles.
Lower your body by bending your elbows and lean slightly forward.
Push your body back up to the starting position.
Chest dips target the chest and triceps.


5.Cable Crossovers:

Stand in front of a cable machine with the cables set to shoulder height.
Hold a handle in each hand and cross your hands over your chest while keeping your arms slightly bent.
Return to the starting position.
This exercise isolates the chest muscles.



6.Decline Bench Press:

Lie on a declined bench (angled downwards) with a barbell or dumbbells.
Lower the weight to your lower chest and push it back up.
This exercise focuses on the lower chest muscles.



7.Incline Dumbbell Flyes:

Lie on an inclined bench with dumbbells in each hand.
Similar to regular flyes but performed on an inclined bench.
Emphasizes the upper chest.



8.Machine Chest Press:

Use a chest press machine.
Sit down and push the handles forward, extending your arms.
Return to the starting position.
This exercise is a controlled alternative to the bench press.
