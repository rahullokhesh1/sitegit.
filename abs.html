Abs Exercises

1.Crunches:

    Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
    Place your hands behind your head or cross them over your chest.
    Lift your upper body off the ground by contracting your abdominal muscles.
    Exhale as you come up and inhale as you lower your upper body back down.
    Avoid pulling on your neck; focus on using your abdominal muscles to lift.

2.Reverse Crunches:

    Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
    Place your hands by your sides or under your hips for support.
    Lift your legs off the ground, bending your knees at a 90-degree angle.
    Contract your lower abdominal muscles to lift your hips off the ground.
    Lower your hips back down without letting your legs touch the floor.

3.Leg Raises:

    Lie flat on your back with your hands under your hips or by your sides.
    Keep your legs straight and lift them toward the ceiling.
    Lower your legs back down, stopping just above the ground without touching it.
    This exercise targets the lower abdominal muscles.

    Alternate

4.Planks:

    Get into a push-up position with your elbows directly beneath your shoulders.
    Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core muscles.
    Hold this position for as long as you can while maintaining proper form.
    Variations include side planks and forearm planks.

5.Russian Twists:

    Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
    Lean back slightly, keeping your back straight, and lift your feet off the ground.
    Hold a weight or medicine ball in your hands.
    Twist your torso to the right and touch the weight to the floor beside your hip.
    Return to the center and then twist to the left, touching the weight to the left side.

6.Mountain Climbers:

    Start in a plank position with your hands under your shoulders.
    Bring one knee toward your chest while keeping the other leg extended.
    Quickly switch legs in a running motion while maintaining the plank position.
    This exercise engages your entire core and also provides a cardiovascular workout.

7.Hanging Leg Raises:

    Hang from the bar with your arms fully extended.
    Lift your legs straight in front of you, raising them as high as you can.
    Lower your legs back down, maintaining control.
    This exercise is more challenging and targets the lower abs.
